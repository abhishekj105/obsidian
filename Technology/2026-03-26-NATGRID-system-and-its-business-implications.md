## What is NATGRID?

The National Intelligence Grid (NATGRID) is an integrated intelligence master database structure for counter-terrorism purposes that connects the databases of various core security agencies under the Government of India. It collects and analyses patterns procured from 21 different organizations, accessible by security agencies round the clock. The project was started in 2009 in the aftermath of the 26/11 Mumbai terror attacks, set up as an attached office of the Ministry of Home Affairs, and started operations on December 31, 2020.

---

## Current Development Status (2025–2026)

NATGRID has undergone a significant leap in scale and capability in the last few months:

**Operational scale:** NATGRID is receiving around 45,000 data requests per month, as central agencies and state police increasingly use the platform for real-time intelligence access.

**Expanded access:** Initially limited to 11 central agencies, access has now expanded to state police units, including officers down to the Superintendent of Police level. This followed a national DGP conference chaired by PM Modi in November 2025.

**NPR integration:** The National Population Register, containing family-level information of 119 crore Indian citizens, was connected to NATGRID in December 2025, enabling safe data exchange between the NIA and Anti-Terrorism Squad.

**Facial recognition at scale:** NATGRID had over 100 crore facial data entries, which rose to 119 crore in 2025 — over 82% of India's total population. Data collected during Covid-19, even while people were wearing masks, is included. Within minutes, NATGRID can access and match masked faces.

**Gandiva — the AI analytics engine:** NATGRID's advanced analytics tool "Gandiva" can be used for facial recognition of criminals and suspects. If the image of a suspect is available, it can be fed into Gandiva, which matches it against photo identity documents like telecom KYC, vehicle registration, or driving licences to surface details — saving investigator time and resources.

**OCND launch:** The Organised Crime Network Database (OCND) — an AI-enabled database working similarly to ChatGPT — was established on 26 December 2025, launched by Home Minister Amit Shah. It gives law enforcement real-time access to FIRs, chargesheets, dossiers, and crime information from every Indian state, plus fingerprint and voice-matching capabilities.

## Implications for Indian Businesses

This is where things get practically significant:

**1. Your data is in the grid.** NATGRID integrates data from 21 government and private organizations across 24+ categories, including banking transactions, travel, telecom, immigration, and more. This means financial institutions, telecom companies, airlines, and any KYC-regulated business are effectively data providers to the system — whether they know it or not.

**2. KYC becomes a national surveillance input.** Telecom KYC, vehicle registration, and bank records are explicitly named as Gandiva-queryable datasets. Any business that collects and stores customer identity data is now part of the intelligence fabric.

**3. Compliance and data governance risk.** The government has notified large parts of the Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023, but many of its provisions will come into force in late 2026 and mid-2027. This creates a compliance grey zone — businesses are sitting on sensitive data that agencies can already query, but consumer protections aren't fully operational yet.

**4. RTI exemption means no transparency.** The government has kept NATGRID outside the RTI ambit — it is included in the second schedule of the RTI Act that exempts intelligence and security organisations. Businesses cannot find out what data about their customers or operations has been queried or flagged.

**5. Social media monitoring.** State authorities have been asked by the MHA to use the platform to access datasets that include information about social media accounts that publish posts about a specific topic. This has implications for brands, public communications, and political speech by business leaders.

**6. Fintech, banking & financial services are most exposed.** FIUs, Enforcement Directorate, and Revenue Intelligence agencies all have NATGRID access. Any unusual financial pattern — even if entirely legal — could trigger a query without the business being notified.

## The Bigger Picture

NATGRID is evolving from a counter-terrorism tool into something considerably broader. Critics argue that without a statutory framework, independent oversight, and meaningful scrutiny, NATGRID is an architecture of suspicion functioning in the service of digital authoritarianism. The linkage with NPR in particular is seen as a shift — grafting a population register onto an intelligence query platform crosses a fundamental boundary, moving from tracking discrete events to mapping every Indian.

For businesses — especially fintechs, real estate platforms, brokerages, and any firm dealing with cross-border transactions or HNI clients — the practical implication is clear: **assume your data trails are visible to the state in real time.** Compliance isn't just about SEBI or RBI anymore; it increasingly intersects with the national security apparatus.
