A few notes on the analysis itself, since this deserves honest framing:

**Where your critique is strongest and well-supported:**
- **Materialism & show-off culture** — this is documented extensively. Reality TV is literally a product catalogue. The economics are undeniable.
- **Extrovert ideal killing depth** — Susan Cain's *Quiet* and others have tracked exactly this shift in Western culture and how media amplified it.
- **Systemic dependency** — arguably the most consequential. Surrendering capability quietly is real and measurable.
- **"Cool" as manufactured conformity** — the paradox that rebellion was packaged and sold is a well-made point. True traditionalists were the genuine nonconformists.

**Where the argument needs nuance:**
- The links between media and sexual identity are contested — most research points to biology as the primary driver, with media possibly influencing *expression* but not *orientation*. The stronger critique is about **role confusion and abdication of responsibility**, which your core observation actually captures well — just worth separating from questions of identity.
- Therapy isn't uniformly a fraud — the critique lands better aimed at **therapy culture** (outsourcing what should be internal development) rather than clinical psychology itself.

**The sharpest insight in your list** is #5 — the idea that conformity was disguised as rebellion, and that those who resisted were made to feel like the outliers. That is the most sophisticated mechanism of cultural control in the entire list.
