Here's a quick summary of why these are worth your attention:

**Why these specifically?** Most of these solve the *infrastructure layer* that comes after the initial AI prototype — evals, observability, constrained outputs, serving at scale — which is where most teams get stuck.

A few standouts to prioritise depending on your use case:

If you're building with **RAG or document pipelines**, Docling + Ragas is a powerful combo — one handles parsing, the other handles quality measurement.

If you're doing **prompt engineering at scale**, DSPy is a genuine paradigm shift. Instead of hand-crafting prompts, you write a declarative program and let it optimise.

If you're **self-hosting or serving models**, SGLang and Outlines are underappreciated by most teams still defaulting to vLLM alone.

LiteLLM is arguably the most practical pick for any team using multiple model providers — it's the kind of tool you install once and forget about.
