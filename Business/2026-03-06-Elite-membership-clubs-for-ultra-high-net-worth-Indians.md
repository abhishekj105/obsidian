Here's a breakdown of the most notable elite groups and private clubs in India catering to people in the ₹50–₹300 crore net worth range:

---

## 🏛️ New-Age Lifestyle Clubs (Soho House equivalents)

**Soho House Mumbai**
The global brand that sparked the trend in India. Members use the space as a haven with a rooftop pool, gym, private screening rooms, and gourmet food — but also to drive value from a diverse community of mentors, investors, and creators. The yearly fee is around ₹1.6 lakh, and it boasts a massive global waitlist.

**The Quorum (Delhi)**
One of the new wave of private membership clubs that has emerged across India's major cities alongside Soho House and BLVD Club in Bangalore. It defines itself as an "urban lifestyle club" and hosts a curated calendar of art collaborations, business forums, and networking events.

**BLVD Club (Bengaluru)**
A newer entrant catering to the Bengaluru tech-entrepreneur crowd, positioned similarly to Soho House in terms of aesthetic and community.

**Club Jolie's (Mumbai)**
Hosts a mix of events ranging from jazz performances to mental health workshops and handwoven textile showcases, blending culture, wellness, and creativity.

---

## 💼 Ultra-HNI Business & Family Office Communities

**Campden (Family Offices)**
Members belong to over 15 of India's most well-heeled families with a net worth of ₹300 crore and above. It prizes confidentiality, with discussions ranging from succession planning, family constitutions, and intergenerational wealth transfer. Very invite-only.

**Priwexus**
A networking forum serving business families, next-generation businesspersons, and first-generation entrepreneurs. Works with those whose net worth ranges from ₹100 crore to ₹300 crore, delving into investments, philanthropy, family governance, and estate management. More accessible than Campden.

**Indus Club (Mumbai, BKC)**
India's answer to Singapore's Tower Club or London's St James — offering 20,000 sq ft of privacy to just 250 members, including a private auditorium, boutique spa, indoor golf simulator, cigar room, and conference rooms. Members automatically gain reciprocal access to elite clubs in London, LA, and beyond.

---

## 🏌️ Traditional Legacy Clubs (Old Money)

These include The Royal Willingdon Sports Club, The Royal Bombay Yacht Club, The Royal Western India Turf Club (RWITC), and The Bay Club — spaces where UHNWIs relax, socialize, and strike business deals. Entry is often by legacy, waiting list, or invitation — sometimes taking decades.

**The Chambers by Taj Hotels** — an ultra-exclusive members lounge within Taj properties, highly curated.

---

## 🌐 Global CEO/Peer Networks

**YPO (Young Presidents' Organization) — Mumbai/Delhi Chapters**
A global network of CEOs and business owners under 45. Very well-regarded for peer learning and deal-making. Net worth and revenue thresholds apply for entry.

**YEO (Young Entrepreneurs' Organization)**
Similar to YPO but slightly more accessible, popular among founders and startup builders in the ₹50–₹200 crore range.

---

## 🏨 Hotel-Backed Exclusive Clubs

**Belvedere at Oberoi Hotels**
Has set its price at ₹15 lakh as a one-time entry fee, followed by ₹1.25 lakh in annual dues — making it one of the more premium hotel-backed clubs.

**Four Seasons and St Regis Private Club Floors** — both have members-only facilities in Mumbai that cater to the ultra-wealthy.

The ₹50–₹300 crore segment is most actively targeted by Priwexus, The Quorum, Indus Club, and BLVD Club.

## 🏠 Soho House Mumbai

**Who gets in:**
Membership policies focus on creativity "above net worth and job titles," with a studied resistance to ostentation. They favour moral values over financial success — reportedly, several executives were turned away for being abusive to staff. The target community is creative professionals — film, fashion, tech, media, music, advertising, art, design.

**Membership types & fees:**
- Membership starts from ₹45,500 quarterly (₹22,750 for Under 27 members)
- For global access (all Soho Houses worldwide), the fee is ₹6 lakh annually
- Cities Without Houses (CWH) membership for Delhi, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Goa, Jaipur, Kolkata costs ~₹3.1 lakh annually — gives access to Soho Houses globally when you travel

**How to apply:**
The application itself is straightforward and takes about 15–20 minutes, but preparation takes around an hour. You choose between a Local House or Every House membership tier. You need:
- 2 existing Soho House member references who can vouch for you
- A profile showcasing your work in a creative field
- A personal statement explaining why you'd add value to the community
- Thorough research on the specific House's culture and demonstrating awareness of Soho House events and initiatives strengthens your application

**Waiting list:** The club boasts a staggering 1 lakh-member waitlist worldwide, so Mumbai specifically can take 1–2+ years unless you have strong member referrals.

**Expansion:** Soho House has announced Soho House Delhi (Mehrauli) and Soho House South Mumbai, with launch dates not yet officially announced.

---

## 🏢 The Quorum (Gurugram, Mumbai, Hyderabad)

**Who gets in:**
Purpose-built for the global citizen — thinkers, doers, and shapers. It brings together a diverse collective of like-minded people and is designed as a meeting of minds. No strict industry requirement unlike Soho House — entrepreneurs, corporate leaders, and creatives are all welcome.

**Fees:**
Standard membership fee averages ₹5 lakh (one-time), plus ₹1.5–2 lakh annual fee. The club has over 3,000 members across its locations.

**Membership types:**
There is an outstation membership (for those living outside the city where a club is located, with limited visit allowances) and an expat membership for those holding foreign passports — both include spouse/partner access.

**How to apply:**
Through their website (thequorumclub.com) — you submit an inquiry and a membership representative contacts you shortly after. It's an interview-based process — they assess your fit with the community rather than just your financials. Member referrals significantly help.

**Perks:** Access to 200 reciprocal clubs worldwide, state-of-the-art fitness centres, and a diverse events calendar.

---

## 🌿 BLVD Club (Bengaluru)

**Who gets in:** Primarily targets Bengaluru's tech entrepreneurs, startup founders, and creative professionals. Less formal in vetting than Soho House but still curated.

**Fees:** Annual subscription starts from ₹6 lakh — making it the priciest of the new-age clubs on an annual basis.

**How to apply:** Via their website (blvdclub.in) — inquiry form followed by a membership team call. Member referrals are preferred. No public waitlist disclosed but capacity is limited.

---

## 🍸 Club Jolie's (Mumbai)

**Who gets in:** Mumbai-based creatives, entrepreneurs, and cultural influencers. Known for its arts and culture programming.

**Fees:** Annual fee of ₹3 lakh.

**How to apply:** Invitation or referral based — you typically need an existing member to introduce you. Direct inquiries can be made but a referral significantly speeds up the process.

---

## 🏛️ The Chambers by Taj Hotels

**Who gets in:** Ultra-HNIs, senior corporate leaders, and established business families. More old-money in character compared to the others.

**Fees:** Membership costs ₹27 lakh with an annual renewal fee of ₹3 lakh — the most premium on this list.

**How to apply:** Strictly by referral from an existing member. No open applications. Taj's team vets profiles directly.

---

## 🔑 Key Tips Across All These Clubs

- **Net worth alone won't get you in.** Cultural fit, professional credibility, and community contribution matter far more, especially at Soho House and The Quorum.
- **Referrals are everything.** Having 1–2 existing members vouch for you cuts waiting times dramatically across all clubs.
- **Attend public/guest events first.** Most clubs host occasional open events — attending these gets you on the radar of the membership team before you formally apply.
- **Profile matters.** A strong LinkedIn, visible work in your field, and media presence (even modest) all help your application.
- Soho House is the hardest to get into due to its global waitlist; The Quorum is relatively more accessible for entrepreneurs and business professionals.

## 🚪 Getting Into Soho House Mumbai Without a Membership

### 1. Dine at Cecconi's (The #1 Hack)

This is your most direct legitimate entry point. Cecconi's, the Northern Italian restaurant on the ground floor of Soho House Mumbai, is available to both members and the public. It's open for lunch and dinner every day, plus breakfast on weekends, with sea-facing views of Juhu Beach.

This is strategically smart because members frequent Cecconi's regularly, and the ground floor naturally mixes members with non-members in a relaxed setting. Soho House members get 20% off the bill, so you'll spot them — they tend to be regulars on weekday evenings. Book via OpenTable or call +91 22 6213 3333.

### 2. Stay as a Hotel Guest

Soho House Mumbai has 38 bedrooms — booking a stay gives you legitimate access to the building and common areas as a hotel guest, though not to members-only floors. It's an expensive but effective way to observe and meet the community.

### 3. Soho Friends Membership (Entry-Level Tier)

The Soho Friends membership gives access to certain areas of the houses and events, and is significantly cheaper than a full membership — useful for those who don't yet qualify for full membership or want to start mixing with members first. This is an official stepping stone toward full membership.

### 4. Cities Without Houses (CWH) Events

The CWH membership connects creative-minded individuals through an exciting programme of events — One Night Only dinners, weekend getaways, music performances, and intimate fashion or art experiences. These are held across Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Goa, Jaipur, and Kolkata. The CWH membership costs ~₹3.1 lakh annually and gives full access to Soho Houses globally when you travel. This is a great way to get embedded in the community before applying for full membership.

### 5. Soho House Mentorship Program

The Soho House Mentorship is a free, 12-week program for creatives aged 21–27 based in Mumbai. Selected mentees receive a year-long membership, access to creative leaders in the network, and professional development workshops. A real foot-in-the-door if you're in that age bracket.

### 6. Be a Guest of a Member

Each member can bring up to 3 guests at a time. Cultivating relationships with existing members — even one — is the most organic way to experience the House and get informally "introduced" to the membership team.

## 🤝 Mumbai's Broader Creative & Entrepreneurial Community

Beyond Soho House itself, these are the ecosystems where its members actually come from and stay active in:

### For Tech Entrepreneurs & Founders

**TEAM — Tech Entrepreneurs Association of Mumbai**
TEAM hosts events like Mumbai Tech Week, Mumbai Hacks, AGI Fridays, and Peak XV Mixers, with co-chairs and members including founders from Dream11, GOQii, upGrad, and BabyChakra. Very high-quality room of operators and investors.

**TiE Mumbai**
One of the oldest and most respected global entrepreneurship networks, with a strong Mumbai chapter in Bandra West. Hosts regular events, mentorship programs, and the annual TiEcon conference — excellent for meeting established business families and investors.

**Startup Grind Mumbai**
Hosts monthly events to teach, inspire, and connect entrepreneurs, with ties to a global community across 600+ cities. More accessible entry point with quality speakers.

**eChai Ventures**
Hosts real startup stories, candid founder conversations, and pitch nights across Mumbai — open to all founders, investors, and product builders. Relaxed, no-gatekeeping format.

### For Creative Professionals (Art, Fashion, Film, Design)

**Lakmé Fashion Week / FDCI events** — Attending shows and after-parties here puts you directly in the same room as Soho House's fashion-world membership.

**Godrej India Culture Lab** — Hosts thought-provoking public talks and cultural events in Mumbai, frequented by the city's intellectual creative class.

**Serendipity Arts Festival (Goa)** — Annual event drawing Mumbai's arts, media, and cultural elite. Strong Soho House overlap in attendees.

**MAMI Film Festival (Mumbai)** — The city's premier film festival, directly aligned with Soho House's film-forward identity. Attending screenings, panels, and parties here is one of the best ways to meet the film-world members.

### For HNI / Business Networking

**YPO Mumbai & EO Mumbai (Entrepreneurs' Organization)** — Peer groups for CEOs and founders with revenue/net worth thresholds. Very well-regarded for serious business introductions.

**Nasscom events & Product Conclave** — India's premier tech industry body, with Mumbai events drawing senior operators and investors.

## 🗺️ The Strategy, Distilled

The smartest path if you want to eventually become a Soho House member looks like this:

1. **Dine at Cecconi's regularly** — become a familiar face
2. **Join CWH membership** (₹3.1L/year) — get into official Soho House events and community
3. **Attend TEAM, TiE, MAMI, or Lakmé events** — build your profile in the community Soho House draws from
4. **Cultivate 2 member friendships** — they become your referees on the application
5. **Apply with a strong creative/professional profile** — by this point, the membership team will likely already know your name
