## ⚠️ Assumption Stress Tests — Where the Model Gets Challenged

The math is clean, but three underlying assumptions deserve scrutiny:

**1. ₹2 Cr all-in cost for a 10,000 sq ft villa**
This implies ₹2,000/sq ft for land + construction combined. Based on the research we did earlier, metro land alone in premium corridors (Whitefield, Kokapet, Gurugram) runs ₹3,000–₹8,000/sq ft. The ₹2 Cr figure is only realistic in Tier-2 cities or semi-rural fringe locations — not in any premium metro market. A realistic metro cost would be ₹5–8 Cr per unit, which compresses margins significantly.

**2. ₹4 Cr selling price for a 10,000 sq ft luxury villa**
At ₹4,000/sq ft selling price, this is at the lower end of the market even in Tier-2 cities. A genuine premium villa with bunker/resilience features in a metro should command ₹8,000–₹15,000/sq ft, meaning ₹8–15 Cr per unit. The model may be underpricing the product — which is actually the upside the document hints at when it mentions the path to ₹5,000 Cr scale.

**3. 14 units/month at ₹4 Cr across 10 parallel projects**
This requires a very well-oiled sales machine. India's luxury villa segment sold roughly 5,000–7,000 units nationwide in 2024, so capturing 168 units/year (~3% of national luxury sales) is ambitious but not impossible for a well-branded concept.
