Here's a complete strategy document + CA brief. Let me walk you through the key layers:

---

**The Core Structural Problem You're Solving**

Banks are skittish about EV two-wheeler loans (65% goes through NBFCs, not banks) because of resale value uncertainty and thin credit profiles of individual 2W buyers. Your advantage: you're not asking for 30 individual loans — you're offering a single corporate borrower with 30 salary-deduction mandates, which effectively makes collection risk zero for the lender.

---

**Four Structuring Models Covered**

**Model A (Recommended)** — Corporate fleet loan in company name, employees sign internal MoUs, EMI recovered via payroll as a "loan recovery" deduction (not a perquisite). Company gets depreciation + potential ITC.

**Model B** — Salary advance to each employee, they buy individually. Simpler banking, but you lose depreciation and ITC. Use only for senior employees with strong individual credit.

**Model C** — Operating lease with a third-party EV fleet lessor (Myles, Vidyuttech, AMU Leasing). Lease rental is 100% deductible, no loan on balance sheet. Cleanest structure for a GST business.

**Model D** — Use the OEM's captive NBFC (Ola Finance, Ather Capital). Fastest execution for 30 units. Negotiate a bulk fleet scheme — they will have one.

---

**On Banking Framework**, the document tells you exactly how to counter the three bank objections (resale risk, no PSL tag, low LTV) and which desks to approach — specifically the **SME/Corporate Fleet desk at SBI or IDFC First**, not retail branches.

---

**The CA Brief (Section 7)** is designed to be detached and sent directly. It covers seven specific questions with section references, including the critical one: ITC on two-wheelers is generally blocked under Section 17(5), but can be claimed if the bikes are used to transport passengers or for business purposes — your inspection engineers' field visits are a strong business-use argument, but your CA needs to formally opine on the documentation trail needed.

One important flag: Section 80EEB, which allowed individuals to claim up to ₹1.5 lakh on interest paid for an EV loan, was applicable only for loans sanctioned between April 1, 2019 and March 31, 2023 — new EV loans taken in 2025-26 are not eligible for this deduction. Don't let employees or your CA factor this in for new loans.
