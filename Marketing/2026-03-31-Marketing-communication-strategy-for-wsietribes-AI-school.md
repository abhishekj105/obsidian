Here's the full direction. Let me walk you through the strategic thinking behind each layer:

**The core reframe.** Your GTM document correctly identifies that you're selling clarity, not courses. The voice framework above locks that in at every touchpoint — the brand archetype is a Wise Mentor, not a salesperson or an alarmist. That distinction is everything.

**The 4 Voice Pillars** are the engine room. Grounded Clarity means no jargon for its own sake. Informed Empathy means you acknowledge the parent's real anxiety before you offer solutions. Outcome Confidence means you speak about who the child *becomes*, not what they study. Earned Authority means you prove, never assert — student stories and real examples over superlatives.

**The tone spectrum** shows where WiseTribe sits — firmly advisory and calm, moderately professional (not corporate), and lightly rational-leaning without losing emotional resonance. This is what separates you from edtech brands that either go full-hustle or full-academic.

**The lexicon** is something to share with anyone writing content — knowing the forbidden words ("world-class", "paradigm shift", "don't fall behind") is as important as knowing the approved ones. The moment you use fear-driven or hollow corporate language, the trust you've built evaporates.

**CTAs** are where most edtech brands lose the plot. Urgency CTAs ("Enrol Now!") signal insecurity. WiseTribe's CTAs name what the parent *receives* — curiosity, a low-stakes first step, proof.

The channel adaptation rules acknowledge that the same message must land differently on Instagram vs LinkedIn vs WhatsApp — not different values, but different registers.
